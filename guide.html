🔄 What is Mondayisation?

As of 1 January 2014, Waitangi Day and Anzac Day became "Mondayised". This means if they fall on a Saturday or Sunday, the following Monday becomes the official day off work.

The holidays are still commemorated and celebrated on their actual dates — Mondayisation only affects which day you get off work.

New Year's Day, Day after New Year's Day, Christmas Day, and Boxing Day have always followed similar transfer rules. If they fall on a weekend, the holiday is observed on the next available working weekday.

For example, if Christmas Day falls on a Saturday, the observed holiday is the following Monday. If Boxing Day also falls on a Sunday, it moves to the Tuesday.

For more information, see the Employment NZ Mondayisation guide.

🎆 New Year's Day & Day After New Year's Day

These two holidays are always on 1 and 2 January. However, if one or both fall on a weekend, the holiday is observed on the next available working weekday.

For example, if New Year's Day is on a Sunday and the Day after New Year's Day is on a Monday, the Day after New Year's takes Monday as usual, and New Year's Day is observed on the Tuesday instead.

Important: If you normally work on a weekend and the holiday falls on your working day, you get that day off — you don't get an additional day.

🇳🇿 Waitangi Day

6 February every year. Waitangi Day commemorates the signing of the Treaty of Waitangi (Te Tiriti o Waitangi) in 1840.

Since 2014, Waitangi Day is Mondayised — if it falls on a weekend, the following Monday is the observed day off. The celebrations still take place on 6 February.

It's also summer in New Zealand, making it a great day to head to the beach with whānau!

🐣 Easter

Easter provides at least two public holidays: Good Friday and Easter Monday. The dates change each year based on a lunisolar calendar.

The date of Easter is determined as the first Sunday after the Paschal Full Moon following the northern hemisphere's vernal equinox (ecclesiastically reckoned as 21 March).

Easter Tuesday

Easter Tuesday is not an official public holiday, but those in the education sector (including schools and universities) typically get the day off. In some regions, it coincides with a regional anniversary day (e.g., Southland Anniversary).

Shop closures: Most shops must be closed on Good Friday and Easter Sunday.

🌺 Anzac Day

25 April every year. Anzac Day commemorates the members of the Australian and New Zealand Army Corps (ANZAC) who served at Gallipoli and other conflicts.

Since 2014, Anzac Day is Mondayised. If it falls on a weekend, you still commemorate it on 25 April, but the day off moves to the following Monday.

Shop closures: Most shops must remain closed until 1pm on Anzac Day.

👑 King's Birthday

The first Monday in June. This isn't actually the King's birthday, but it's a welcome long weekend regardless!

Previously known as Queen's Birthday, the name changed following the accession of King Charles III.

✨ Matariki

Matariki (Ngā Mata o te Ariki Tāwhirimātea) is the Māori name for a cluster of stars (the Pleiades) and marks the beginning of the Māori new year.

Matariki became a public holiday in 2022 — the first new public holiday in New Zealand in decades. The date varies each year but always falls on a Friday in June or July, based on the Māori lunar calendar.

Matariki is a time to:

  • Remember those who have passed
  • Celebrate the present with whānau and friends
  • Plan for the year ahead

The Friday placement ensures a long weekend for all workers — a welcome change from Mondayisation which tends to favour Monday-to-Friday workers.

💪 Labour Day

The fourth Monday in October. Labour Day celebrates the achievements of New Zealand workers and the historic 8-hour working day movement. Since it always falls on a Monday, there's no Mondayisation confusion!

🎄 Christmas Day & Boxing Day

25 & 26 December. These follow the same transfer rules as New Year's Day and the Day after New Year's Day.

Because Christmas and New Year are exactly 7 days apart, whatever Mondayisation rules apply to Christmas will also apply to New Year's Day the following week.

Shop closures: Most shops must be closed on Christmas Day.

📍 Regional Anniversary Days

Each provincial region in New Zealand has a designated anniversary day. If the actual date falls on a weekend, it's typically moved to a nearby Monday or Friday for the observed day off.

Auckland Anniversary

Actual date: 29 January. Observed on the Monday closest to 29 January. Covers Auckland, Northland, Waikato, King Country, Coromandel, Bay of Plenty, Gisborne and East Coast.

Wellington Anniversary

Actual date: 22 January. Observed on the Monday closest to 22 January. Covers Wellington, Whanganui, Palmerston North, Kapiti, Feilding, Levin and Masterton.

Nelson Anniversary

Actual date: 1 February. Observed on the Monday closest to 1 February.

Taranaki Anniversary

Actual date: 31 March. Observed on the second Monday of March. Covers Taranaki region including Inglewood, Waitara, Hawera, Stratford and Eltham.

Otago Anniversary

Actual date: 23 March. Observed on the Monday closest to 23 March.

Southland Anniversary

Actual date: 17 January. Observed on Easter Tuesday (decided by the mayors of Invercargill, Southland and Gore in December 2011).

Canterbury Anniversary

Actual date: 16 December. Observed on the second Friday after the first Tuesday in November (also Christchurch Show Day). Covers North and Central Canterbury including Christchurch and Ashburton.

South Canterbury Anniversary

Actual date: 16 December. Observed on the fourth Monday in September. This coincides with Dominion Day — the anniversary of NZ gaining Dominion status (1907).

Hawke's Bay Anniversary

Actual date: 1 November. Observed on the Friday before Labour Day.

Marlborough Anniversary

Actual date: 1 November. Observed on the first Monday after Labour Day.

Westland Anniversary

Actual date: 1 December. Observed on the Monday closest to 1 December. The date can vary outside of Greymouth.

Chatham Islands Anniversary

Actual date: 30 November. Observed on the Monday closest to 30 November.

🏪 Shop Opening Rules

Most shops are required to be closed on:

  • Good Friday
  • Easter Sunday
  • Christmas Day
  • Anzac Day morning (until 1pm)

Some exemptions apply for dairies, service stations, pharmacies, restaurants, cafés, and shops in tourist areas. Check with your local council for specific rules.

🕐 Daylight Saving

Daylight Saving starts in spring on the last Sunday in September. Clocks go forward from 2:00am to 3:00am — "Spring forward!"

Daylight Saving ends in autumn on the first Sunday in April. Clocks go back from 3:00am to 2:00am — "Fall back!"

An easy way to remember: "Spring forward, Fall (Autumn) back."
